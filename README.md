# Casino

Verdi White family card game played with Rook cards

## 1. Overview

*Casino* is a team-based card game derived from [Cassino][C] and played using [Rook][R] cards.
Players capture, build, and control piles of cards to score points.
The game involves strategy, teamwork, and timing.

[C]: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cassino_(card_game)
[R]: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rook_(card_game)

## 2. Players and Setup

### Players

* 2–6 players usually played in **teams of 2**
* Teammates sit **across from each other**

### Decks

* Use **N Rook decks**, where **N = number of teams**
* Shuffle all decks together into a single draw pile

Traditionally, the cards are shuffled by spreading them out and mixing them by hand in a circular motion.

### Chips (Scoring)

* White chip = 1 point
* Red chip = 10 points
* Blue chip = 50 points

Each team starts with **2 red chips and 10 white chips**.

### Card Values

* Red 10s = 10 points
* Black 2s = 5 points
* Black cards = 1 point
* Rook cards = 1 point
* All other cards = 0 points

## 3. Objective

Score the most points by:

* Capturing cards
* Controlling piles
* Earning bonuses

## 4. Turn Structure

### Start of Game

* Each player turns up one card from the draw pile
* The player with the highest number goes first
* Ties for highest card are broken by drawing additional cards until a single player has the highest.

### Each Round

* Each player draws **4 cards**
* Players take turns clockwise

Note: For the final round, if fewer than four cards remain per player, each player draws 1–3 additional cards, and any remaining cards are turned face-up on the table.

### Each Turn

Play **one card** by doing any number of the following:

1. Capture cards
2. Build a pile
3. Add to an existing pile
4. Play a card to the table

## 5. Capturing Cards

All captured cards are placed **face-up** in your team's pile to distinguish them from the draw pile.

### Matching

* Play a card to capture one or more cards of the **same number**

### Combining

* Play a card whose value equals the **sum of multiple table cards**
* Example: A 7 captures a 2 + 5

## 6. Building Piles

### Creating a Build

* Combine cards on the table into a **declared total**
* You must have a card in your hand equal to that total

Example:

* Declare a build of 14
* Combine cards such as 6 + 8 or 10 + 4

### Adding to a Build

* You or your partner may add cards to the build
* All additions must preserve the declared total

## 7. Freezing Piles

### Freezing a Build

* An opposing player may **freeze** a build if they hold a card matching the declared total
* To freeze a build, the player must add cards consistent with that total
* When frozen, only that team may capture the build

### Re-freezing

* The original team may **freeze back** by adding additional cards consistent with the total

### Capturing a Build

* In the end, a build must be captured using a card equal to the declared total

## 8. Penalties

### Cannot Play

* If the first player in a round plays a card to the table (instead of capturing or building),
  their team must pay **each other team** N points, where N is the number of cards currently on the table.

### Lost Cards

* If you play a **black 2** and another team captures it, you must pay the other team **5 points**.
* If you play a **red 10** and another team captures it, you must pay the other team **10 points**.
* If you play a **Rook** and another team captures it, you must pay the other team **20 points**.

## 9. Bonus Points

### Sweeps

* If you capture all cards on the table, each other team pays your team **1 point**.

### Special Hands

After drawing 4 cards, you may earn from the bank:

* All cards the same color → **+5 points**
* All cards the same number → **+N points**, where N is the card number

Note: The Rook is wild and may represent any number or color.
If used for a number bonus, it must be declared as that number and played accordingly

### Poverty

* All cards ≤ 4 → +5 points from the bank
* All cards ≤ 3 → +10 points from the bank
* All cards ≤ 2 → +15 points from the bank
* All cards ≤ 1 → +20 points from the bank

### Most Cards

At the end of the game, the team with the most captured cards earns **1 point per card more** than the second-highest team. For example:

* Team A: 70 cards
* Team B: 58 cards
* Team C: 43 cards
* Team A earns **12 points** (70 - 58) from the bank

## 10. Winning the Game

* Teams total all points (chips + bonuses)
* The team with the highest score wins

### Match Play (Recommended)

Because going first can be disadvantageous:

* Play multiple games equal to the number of players
* Rotate the starting player clockwise each game
* Combine scores across all games to determine the final winner

Example:

* 4 players → play 4 matches and sum all scores
* 6 players → play 3 matches (one per team) and sum all scores
